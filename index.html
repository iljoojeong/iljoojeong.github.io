© Iljoo Jeong

I am a Senior Researcher at the Korea Electronics Technology Institute (KETI), working on physics-grounded industrial AI for manufacturing, sensing, and physical system understanding. My research focuses on recovering hidden physical states from incomplete and indirect observations — acoustic signals, sensor streams, wafer maps, and process data — and translating them into diagnosis, prediction, and optimization for real-world industrial systems. I hold a Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from POSTECH, conducted research at CMU and KRISS, and bring industry experience from ASML and Mando.

Research Overview

Physics-Grounded Industrial AI

Embedding physical priors (wave equations, dynamics, material laws) into neural architectures for physically consistent, data-efficient predictions — the common core running through every theme below.

Methods: INN · GNN · Conditional Generative Models

Acoustic & Multimodal Inverse Problems

Recovering latent physical states from indirect observations — sound-source localization, room-geometry reconstruction, radar-based human sensing, and ultrasonic imaging.

Works: IEEE TIM 2024 · IEEE/ACM TASLP 2024 · KRISS 2021–25

Manufacturing Diagnostics & Process Optimization

Deep-learning diagnostics and quality prediction for real manufacturing — from wafer-map failure patterns to machining processes and machine health.

Works: Wafer-map defect classification · Milling optimization · Saw-blade wear

Physical AI toward Autonomous Manufacturing

Extending physics-grounded sensing and diagnostics toward autonomous decision-making in production systems — simulation, state estimation, and control in the loop.

Works: PHM Korea 2026 · Aircraft-manufacturing logistics (PI)
